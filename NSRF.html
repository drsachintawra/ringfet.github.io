Nanoscale RingFET

Fig 2.01 (a)-(b) shows different orientation views of bulk MOSFET and RingFET architecture respectively to illustrate the difference in the device architectures. It can be seen from the figure that, RingFET architecture can be obtained by rotating the bulk MOSFET along the vertical direction as shown in Fig. 2.01 (a)-(b), i.e. gate is actively present only over the channel (as in case of bulk MOSFET and the gate is in circular form). In addition to this, the active source/drain area is asymmetric in RingFET depending upon the position of drain i.e. either inside the channel or outside as shown in Fig 2.01 (c)-(d). Thus, RingFET architecture also comes into the category of single gate devices i.e. single gate bulk MOSFET having circular geometry as depicted in Fig. 2.01 (b). Due to circular architecture of the source/drain it shows numerous advantages over other available FET architecture such as suppressed floating-body effects, improved transport property, excellent CMOS compatibility [Ghosh12b, Wang07] and better hot carrier reliability.
