Double Gate RingFET (DG-RingFET)

Fig 4.01 (a)-(c) and (d)-(f) shows the different orientation views of Cylindrical FET and RingFET architecture respectively to explain the difference between the RingFET architecture and conventional cylindrical MOSFET. Figure 4.01 (a)-(f) Showing the comparison between DG-RingFET and Cylindrical Gate FET architecture [adapted from [Kumar17a]. It can be seen from the Fig. 4.01 (a)-(c) that, cylindrical/surrounding FET is obtained by rotating the DG- MOSFET along the horizontal direction as shown in Fig. 4.01 (a) whereas DG-RingFET can be obtained by rotating the planer DG-MOSFET along the vertical direction as shown in Fig. 4.01 (d). Thus, it is clear from the figures that the two architectures i.e. cylindrical FET and DG-RingFET are different in orientation and gate controllability aspect. Since in cylindrical FET, the gate is wrapped around the channel but in DG-RingFET, gate is actively present only over the channel (i.e. on top and bottom) as in case of DG-MOSFET. As explained in the previous chapter regarding asymmetric behavior of RingFET, DG-RingFET also shows asymmetric nature i.e. source/drain regions are interchangeable and show different properties for asymmetric geometry cases. The device characteristics are strongly dependent on the position of drain source region (i.e. inside the ring or outside). This relative difference in size becomes significant at nanoscale (i.e. under sub 50 nm regime). Hence, drain current characteristic of RingFET depends on inner and outer drain as well as channel length (L) and drain radii (RD).
