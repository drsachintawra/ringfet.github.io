About Project

Computational module facilitates the researcher to perform multiple simulated experiments by using computer in order to have a better understanding of system and it also helps user save lot of time. A computational module contains number of variables, which helps user in characterizing the system under consideration. Key feature of a computational model is that it allows the user to study all electrical, electronic as well as biological systems. In our work we have focused on computational module for studying basic building blocks of VLSI industry i.e. the microelectronic devices like MOSFET's. In this work a web-based platform has been created where researchers can contribute their novel device designs and computational modules. A simulation engine has also been built in to the platform; this enables the users (community) to interactively browse the characteristics of the novel device designs published on the platform. Currently, the platform facilitates the user with characterization and analysis of Nanoscale RingFET. User can investigate basic characteristics such as Surface Potential and Drain Current of Nanoscale RingFET and can also evaluate the impact of various dimensional and physical parameters like Channel Length (Lch), Gate Bias (Vgs), Drain Bias (Vds), Substrate Doping (NA), Source/Drain Doping (ND), Gate Metal Work-function (Φm) etc. It is envisaged that this platform will offer a conducive medium of collaboration for researchers enabling them to interactwith their peers and community and receive feedback on their work, while it makes it simpler for other users to understand the work and provide feedback. It shall also allow students across Engineering, Applied Science and other disciplines to brush up their VLSI design fundamentals by performing interactive simulations of devices as they study them in theory and keep them abreast of the latest advancements in the field.

Platform Design

The platform has been designed using web2.0 technologies. The frontend is powered by HTML5/CSS3 and JavaScript, while the backend – the core processing and analytics unit – is implemented in PYTHON.

Analytical Model

The mathematical equations for analytical model were obtained by solving Poisson's equation in the channel region of the device to obtain its surface potential. After this, these equations were coded in python using math and other libraries of python. Numerical methods like Newton Raphson and Fourier Bessel series were implemented to solve algebraic and calculus functions.
