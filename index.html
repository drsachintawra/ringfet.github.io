Moore's Law

The idea of a technology roadmap for semiconductors was discussed in an article reported by Gordon E. Moore (Intel Co-founder) in 1965, where it is stated that the number of components that could be incorporated per integrated circuit would increase exponentially over time [Arden14, Schwierz10]. This would result in the reduction of manufacturing cost per function while enabling the production of more complex circuits on a single semiconductor substrate. Since 1970, the number of components per chip has doubled every two years. This historical trend is known as â€• Moore's Law [ITRS14].
